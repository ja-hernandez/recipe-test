Sweet Potato Pie

Ingredients Amount
medium sweet potatoes 3-4
butter, softened 1/2 stick
white sugar 1/4 cup
brown sugar 3/4 cup
eggs 2
vanilla extract 1 teaspoon
ground cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon
ground ginger 1/8 teaspoon
ground nutmeg 1/8 teaspoon
ground allspice 1/8 teaspoon
salt 1/2 teaspoon
evaporated milk 3/4 cup
unbaked pie crust 1 9" uncooked
  1. Preheat oven to 350 degree F
  2. Wrap sweet potatoes in foil and bake for 1 hour in the oven.
  3. When done, let cool. Scrape the pulp out of the skin, transfer to a large bowl, and mash. Set aside. Should yield 2 cups mashed sweet potato
  4. In a medium bowl, beat together butter, sugar, and brown sugar until creamy.
  5. Beat eggs and add to bowl along with vanilla, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, allspice, salt, and evaporated milk. Stir to combine.
  6. Add sweet potatoes to mixture and beat until smooth. Pour into pie crust
  7. Bake for 1 hour, making sure center of pie is firm but not burnt

Yields 9" Pie. No serving suggestion needed

Cushaw (squash) Pie

Ingredients Amount
cushaw squash 2 1/2 cups roasted, mashed
heavy cream 1/4 cup
eggs, beaten 2
all-purpose flour (not self-rising) 1 Tablespoon
white sugar 1 cup
ground nutmeg 1/4 teaspoon
ground allspice 1/4 teaspoon
salt 1/2 teaspoon
lemon extract 1/2 teaspoon
butter, melted 2 tablespoons
unbaked pie crust 1 9" uncooked
  1. Preheat oven to 350 degree F
  2. Cut off neck of squash, then slice in half lengthwise. Scrape out seeds. Bake on baking sheet for 1 hour. Squash should become soft
  3. When done, let cool. Scrape the flesh out of the skin, transfer to a large bowl, and mash
  4. Raise temperature of oven to 450 F
  5. Using 2 1/2 cups of cooked cushaw, add heavy cream and stir
  6. Add beaten eggs. Mix well and add sugar, flour, salt, nutmeg, and allspice; mix well to blend. Blend in lemon extract and butter.
  7. Pour into pie crust and bake at 450 for 10 min, then lower to 350
  8. Bake for another 40-50 min, making sure pie sets and toothpick comes out clean

Yields 9" Pie. No serving suggestion needed
